Consignment Agreement

WHEREFORE, owner and Consignor (hereinafter referred to as “Consignor”) of certain tickets more particularly described below, and ROCKSTAR SEATING LLC (hereinafter referred to as “Consignee”) do agree to the following set forth in this Consignment Agreement (“Agreement”):

  1. Ownership: Consignor warrants, covenants, and agrees that the ticket(s), being sold is/are owned by Consignor and that there are no liens, judgments, or other encumbrances against ownership and validity, including all rights of sale or transfer, of the consigned tickets. The parties agree that the title to the consignment shall remain with Consignor until such consignment is sold in severable parts or in whole by or to Consignee.
  2. Description & Validity of Consigned Tickets: Consignor agrees to deliver, and Consignee agrees to accept for the purpose of sale on the terms and conditions hereinafter recited in this Agreement any and all tickets that are submitted as Consignment Inventory via our website www.RockstarSeating.com, or by any other means of communication.
    Consignor states and agrees that the description(s) of the ticket(s) is true and correct to the best of Consignor's knowledge and belief and further, that no undisclosed defects ion such consignment are known to Consignor. In addition, Consignor warrants and guarantees that they are the sole and only owner of the tickets and that the tickets have been completely paid for upon delivery to Consignee. Consignor assumes all liability in the event that ticket(s) are not genuine or valid and understands they will be 100% responsible for all damages and replacement costs of like or better ticket(s) to Consignee's customers.
  3. Non-exclusivity or Exclusive Agreement: Consignee shall have the exclusive right to market and sell the tickets associated with this Agreement. Consignor shall have the right to sell the property described hereinabove for his/her own account provided that Consignee does not have a pending sale or has not already sold the ticket(s)
  4. Price: Consignee can offer its professional advice regarding pricing and current market trends; however the Consignor has complete control and responsibility over the sale price of the ticket(s).
  5. Amount of Payments to Consignee: Consignor and Consignee agree that an amount of TBD% (referred to as ‘Consignment Rate’) of the net selling price (total sale less any and all fees associated with the cost of the sale) shall be due and payable to Consignee upon sale of the ticket(s) subject to Agreement.
  6. Sale of Ticket(s): Consignee will engage in all efforts toward achieving the goal of selling the ticket(s) on behalf of the Consignor, however Consignee makes no warranties or guarantees that Consignor's ticket(s) will sell. Further, Consignee assumes no liability for ticket(s) that go unsold. The Consignor is responsible for all costs related to shipping or delivering the tickets to and from Consignee.
  7. Consignor's Replacement Cost Responsibility: In the event Consignor cannot produce the ticket(s) upon its sale to a third party purchaser by the Consignee, Consignor is fully responsible for any and all replacement costs, including, but not limited to, monetary and administrative costs incurred by the Consignee associated with having to honor the sale to the third party purchaser. Consignor agrees to provide a valid credit card number at the time of Consignment to guarantee their ticket listing(s) for resale. In the event that the credit card number on file is invalid, Consignor acknowledges that they will be further invoiced for such replacement costs, if applicable.
  8. Termination of this Consignment Agreement: Consignee may terminate this Agreement at any time and for any reason by returning to Consignor all of the unsold ticket(s) which is subject to this Agreement. Consignor may terminate this Agreement at any time and for any reason, however such termination will not affect the Agreement regarding ticket(s) already sold or pending sale. The expense of delivery to the Consignor of any unsold ticket(s) shall be paid by the Consignor.
  9. Act of Nature / God / Unforeseen Circumstance: In the chance that an event is cancelled for any reason Consignor shall refund Consignee the proceeds from sale in order to refund the Consignee's customers.
  10. Theft or Destruction of Consignor's Ticket(s): In the event of theft of destruction of Consignor's ticket(s), while the ticket(s) are in the possession of the Consignee, Consignor agrees to first approach the original ticket office issuing the ticket(s), or work in conjunction with Consignee and original ticket office, in procuring reprinted tickets before and replacement costs for the stolen or destroyed ticket(s) shall be paid out by Consignee.
  11. Operation of Consignee's Business: Consignee shall have the exclusive right to determine the business operation and management of its premises. Consignor shall have liability or responsibility for the operation and management of Consignee's business, employees, or agents and any torts or other causes of action resulting there from.
  12. Liability for Loss of Tickets: In the event that consigned property is lost, stolen, destroyed, damaged, or unaccounted for due to any cause, known or unknown (hereinafter refereed to collectively as “lost”), while consigned to the Consignee:
    The ticket(s) so shall lost shall be considered as having been sold, and Consignee shall pay unto Consignor fair market value less the approved Consignment Rate of said amount (rather than the full amount) shall be owed by Consignee to Consignor. Each amount shall be due within 10 business days following the loss of the ticket(s).
  13. Advertising: Consignee will not use Consignor's name or other identifying information in the advertising, promotion, and sale of the consigned tickets. Consignor should be aware that some tickets print the name of the original cardholder on the ticket itself – Consignee shall not be held responsible for the identifying information presented on the original ticket consigned through this Agreement.
  14. Assignment: This Agreement is not assignable and may not be modified other than by a written modification agreed to and signed by both parties.
  15. Construction: This Agreement shall be construed and governed according to the laws of the State of Washington.
  16. Waiver: The parties agree that instances or patterns, or waiver, forbearance, course of dealing or trade usage shall not affect the right of a party to demand performance of any term or condition of this Agreement.
